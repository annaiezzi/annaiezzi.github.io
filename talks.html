  • 06/12/2019: An application of the Hasse-Weil bound to rational functions over finite fields, Arithmetic, Geometry, Cryptography, and Coding Theory 2019 (AGC^2T-17), CIRM, Marseille, France

    An application of the Hasse-Weil bound to rational functions over finite fields

    The Hasse–Weil bound has many applications in the arithmetic of finite fields. Indeed, in the study of polynomial equations over finite fields, it represents a powerful tool for proving asymptotic claims, i.e. when the cardinality of the finite field is sufficiently large. Examples of such asymptotic results appear, for instance, in the literature about permutation polynomials over finite fields.

    In this talk we will show how we can use the Hasse–Weil bound for proving results about rational functions over finite fields. For instance, given $f(x)$, $g(x)\in \mathbb F_q(x)$, we will discuss the conditions under which there exists $h(x)\in\mathbb F_q(x)$ such that $f(x)=g(h(x))$. This is a joint work with Xiang-dong Hou.

  • 12/11/2018: A note on the security of CSIDH, Indocrypt 2018, New Delhi, India.

    A note on the security of CSIDH

    We propose a quantum algorithm for computing an isogeny between two elliptic curves $E_1,E_2$ defined over a finite field such that there is an imaginary quadratic order $\mathcal O$ satisfying $\mathcal O\simeq \operatorname{End}(E_i)$ for $i = 1,2$. This concerns ordinary curves and supersingular curves defined over $\mathbb F_p$ (the latter used in the recent CSIDH proposal).

    Our algorithm has heuristic asymptotic run time $e^{O\left(\sqrt{\log(|\Delta|)}\right)}$ and requires polynomial quantum memory and $e^{O\left(\sqrt{\log(|\Delta|)}\right)}$ quantumly accessible classical memory, where $\Delta$ is the discriminant of $\mathcal O$. This asymptotic complexity outperforms all other available methods for computing isogenies.

  • 02/09/2019: Isogeny-based cryptography, a quantum-safe alternative, Florida Women In Mathematics Day, Florida Atlantic University, Boca Raton.

    Isogeny-based cryptography, a quantum-safe alternative

    Since Peter Shor's discovery in 1994 of a quantum polynomial-time algorithm for integer factorization, the cryptographic community has been working hard to design new cryptosystems that could face the cryptanalytic powers of quantum computers. Isogeny-based cryptography is currently considered to be one of these quantum-safe alternatives.

    In this talk we will present a class of isogeny-based cryptosystems based on a similar scheme, and we will discuss the ``hard" mathematical problem that underlies the security of these systems.

  • 19/11/2018: On the security of a recent isogeny-based cryptosystem, Discrete Mathematics Seminar, Department of Mathematics & Statistics, University of South Florida.

    On the security of a recent isogeny-based cryptosystem

    Because of their arithmetic properties, since the 1980s elliptic curves have played a crucial role in several public-key cryptosystems. Among these, the most recent ones, called \emph{isogeny-based cryptosystems}, use the notion of isogeny, a particular kind of map, between elliptic curves, and have been introduced in order to face the cryptanalytic powers of quantum computers. The security of these cryptosystems is based on the difficulty of computing an isogeny between two given isogenous elliptic curves.

    In this seminar, after reviewing the fundamental mathematics of isogeny-based cryptography, I will discuss the security of the recent isogeny-based cryptosystem CSIDH proposed by Castryck et al. This will be also an opportunity to do some algebraic number theory and graph theory.

    This is a joint work with Jean-François Biasse and Michael J. Jacobson.

  • 03/05/2018: A singular trip through the world of algebraic curves over finite fields, Discrete Mathematics Seminar, Department of Mathematics & Statistics, University of South Florida.

    A singular trip through the world of algebraic curves over finite fields

    For an algebraic curve defined over a finite field $\mathbb F_q$, the number of rational points, i.e. of points of the curve with coordinates in $\mathbb F_q$, is always finite. Due to this discrete aspect, algebraic curves over finite fields have found, in the last 40 years, several applications in cryptography and coding theory.

    In this talk, we will explore this problem of counting points, by showing the fundamental tools and results that appear in this theory. In particular, we will see how the case of smooth curves generalizes to the case of singular ones and we will present a construction of singular curves with many rational points.

  • 11/20/2016: Curve su campi finiti a singolarità prescritte, Seminario d'Algebra Commutativa, Dipartimento di Matematica e Fisica, Università degli Studi di Roma Tre.

    Curve su campi finiti a singolarità prescritte

    Nella teoria delle curve definite su campi finiti, lo studio del numero di punti razionali riveste un ruolo fondamentale, anche in virtù delle numerose applicazioni, in teoria dell’informazione, alla crittografia e alla teoria dei codici. Tale tema è stato ampiamente trattato nel caso di curve lisce, vedendo come attori principali Hasse, Weil e Serre. Noi ci interesseremo al caso più generale di curve singolari. Più precisamente, dopo aver richiamato gli oggetti e i risultati primari che intervengono nella teoria delle curve su campi finiti, ci concentreremo su una costruzione, basata su nozioni e metodi propri dell’algebra commutativa, di curve a singolarità prescritte. Tale costruzione permetterà di dimostrare alcuni risultati sul numero massimo di punti razionali di una curva definita su un dato campo finito e di fissati genere geometrico e aritmetico.

  • 11/05/2015: Courbes optimales et maximales, Séminaire d’Arithmétique et Théorie de l’Information, Institut de Mathématiques de Marseille, Aix-Marseille Université.

    Courbes optimales et maximales

    En utilisant une approche euclidienne, nous prouverons une nouvelle borne sur le nombre de points fermés de degrés 2 sur une courbe lisse définie sur le corps fini $\mathbb F_q$. Cette borne nous permettra de donner des conditions explicites sur q, g et $\pi$ pour la non-existence d'une courbe définie sur $\mathbb F_q$ de genre géométrique g et genre arithmétique $\pi$ avec $N_q(g)+\pi-g$ points rationnels. De plus, pour q carré, nous étudierons le spectre des genres des courbes maximales définies sur $\mathbb F_q$, c'est à dire l'ensemble des couples $(g,\pi)$ pour lesquels il existe une courbe définie sur $\mathbb F_q$ de genre géométrique $g$ et genre arithmétique $\pi$ avec $q+1+2g\sqrt{q}+\pi-g$ points rationnels.
