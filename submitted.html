    Explicit connections between supersingular isogeny graphs and Bruhat-Tits treess

    In this paper we study the connections between supersingular isogeny graphs and quotients of Bruhat-Tits trees. Bruhat-Tits trees have a natural orientation and notion of distance and depth which could be useful for cryptanalysis of cryptosystems based on supersingular isogeny graphs. Vertices and edges of Bruhat-Tits trees also have a very simple representation as matrices, and we show how to explicitly compute the corresponding elliptic curves and isogenies.
